The Growth Factor in Church Tithing

Achieving Growth in our Personal Relations with God in Church Tithing

As the father of the Church, Abraham’s experience of growth in his personal relations with God, following his tithing is what gives us the benchmark to seek growth in our personal relations with God, in Church tithing. What powered Abraham into receiving the growth experience in his personal relations with God, following his tithing?

Many of us have not considered the spiritual and the sacred context that powered Abraham to give his tithes, and then, as an outcome, receive the experience of growth in his personal relations with God. What was this spiritual and sacred context?

1 First, let’s note that as the priest of God, Melchizedek represented God in his engagement with Abraham (Genesis 14:18), and that characterizes their meeting as a spiritual and a sacred engagement between God and Abraham. 2 Furthermore, Melchizedek brought forth bread and wine (Genesis 14:18), which means he had a fellowship of communion with Abraham. 3 A third thing to note is that Melchizedek declared to Abraham the word of God concerning God’s personal influence in the winning of the battle against the kings (Genesis 14:19-20), which unveiled not only the personal nature of God to Abraham, but also, that God can be related to, personally. 4 Fourthly, based on Abraham’s response to the king of Sodom (Genesis 14:21-23), we are to note that Melchizedek dedicated him to God, and received his pledge to distance himself from the king of Sodom, who perhaps symbolizes the lustful things of the world.

The above items give us the spiritual and sacred things that Abraham was involved with, prior to, or in the context of the giving of his tithes. It appears by Melchizedek, that Abraham was engaged in a type of devotional activity with God, before he gave the tithes, or in the process of giving his tithes. The nature of the devotional activity with God unveiling His personal nature to Abraham would impact his personal relations with God to give him the growth experience in his trust (Genesis 24:40, Romans 4:21, Hebrews 11:17-19), love (Genesis 22:12), and holiness to God (Genesis 17:1), as well as the integrity of the personal presence and influence of God in his life (Genesis 24:40).

We see here that Abraham’s tithing was connected to a devotional activity with God to give him growth in his personal relations with Him. In making Abraham the father of the Church, God was also benchmarking with him the spiritual and the sacred approach that Church tithers are to give to the practice of Church tithing; that would result in growth in their personal relations with Him. It is this understanding of God’s work in Abraham’s tithing that gives us the framework for ‘Tithing to Grow in Christ’.

Tithing to Grow in Christ is an approach to Church tithing that situates church tithers into the discipline of having a devotional time with God, before they give their tithes at church, with the goal of growing in their personal relations with God, by having fulfillments in the Five Christian Persuasions of Church Tithing.

The diligence we give to the ritual of having a quality devotional time with God regarding the Five Christian Persuasions of Church Tithing, prior to the giving of our tithes at church is how we make our tithing count for growth in our trusting and loving relations with God; all together allowing that diligence to impact our Christian formation.
